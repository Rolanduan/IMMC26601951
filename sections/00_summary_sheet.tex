\textbf{\Large Problem Understanding}

Large national parks face critical conservation challenges: protecting wildlife across vast, geographically complex areas with limited personnel and resources. Traditional uniform coverage approaches are infeasible. We develop a strategic resource allocation framework for \textbf{Etosha National Park} (Namibia) that prioritizes high-value conservation targets while maintaining robust protection under uncertainty.

\vspace{0.4cm}

\textbf{\Large Our Approach}

We propose a multi-layer optimization framework integrating:

\noindent \textbf{1. Quantifiable Protection Metrics (AHP + Fuzzy Evaluation):} Defined protection score $P_i = \sum_{j=1}^{4} w_j \cdot r_{ij}$ with AHP-derived weights $\mathbf{w} = (0.50, 0.30, 0.15, 0.05)$ for ecology, threat, operations, and seasonality. Priority zones identified by protection gap $G_i = 0.85 - P_i$.

\noindent \textbf{2. Integer Programming Core:} Maximize $\sum_{i} w_i P_i(x_i)$ subject to budget, personnel, and coverage constraints using mixed-integer linear programming (MILP) formulation with binary deployment variables.

\noindent \textbf{3. Spatial-Temporal Allocation:} Strategic deployment of ranger patrols, UAVs, and camera traps using graph-based accessibility modeling and dynamic programming for multi-period planning.

\noindent \textbf{4. Queueing-Based Staffing Inference:} M/M/c queue model determines minimum staff from response time targets: $c_{min} = \lceil \lambda / (\mu \cdot \rho_{target}) \rceil = 127$ rangers with 3-shift rotation.

\noindent \textbf{5. Robustness Under Uncertainty:} Monte Carlo simulation (10,000 trials) with 95\% confidence intervals and game-theoretic Stackelberg analysis for adversarial deterrence.

\noindent \textbf{Key Findings:}
\begin{itemize}
\item Strategic allocation achieves 27-36 percentage point improvement in critical protection metrics
\item Queueing analysis: $P(\text{wait}=0) = 81.8\%$, mean waiting time $W_q = 25.8$ minutes
\item Monte Carlo robustness: System protection $90.8\% \pm 2.4\%$, 95\% CI $[86.2\%, 94.9\%]$
\item Game-theoretic deterrence: 88.0\% in high-priority zones, 65.0\% in medium-priority zones
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.4cm}

\textbf{\Large Key Recommendations}

\noindent \textbf{1.} Adopt priority-based deployment: 70\% of resources to high-priority zones (water holes, endangered species habitats)

\noindent \textbf{2.} Implement hybrid monitoring: Ranger patrols for high-threat areas, drones for inaccessible terrain, camera traps for continuous surveillance

\noindent \textbf{3.} Staff at 127 rangers with 3-shift rotation for continuous coverage while maintaining target protection level

\noindent \textbf{4.} Establish dynamic reallocation protocol for seasonal threat variations (poaching spikes, dry season wildlife concentration)

\noindent \textbf{5.} Framework transferable to reserves on different continents with minimal structural modification
