\textbf{\Large Problem Understanding}

We address routing optimization for humanitarian aid delivery in Somalia, where travel times are highly uncertain due to seasonal weather (rainy seasons cause flooding), security incidents (conflict zones), and poor infrastructure (mix of paved and dirt roads).

\noindent \textbf{Task A (Single Warehouse):} Optimize delivery routes from central warehouse to distribution points, vehicles return to warehouse. Minimize expected time while ensuring route reliability $\geq 85\%$.

\noindent \textbf{Task B (Multiple Warehouses):} Extend to multiple local warehouses with coordinated delivery strategies, service area partitioning, and inter-warehouse inventory transfers.

\vspace{0.4cm}

\textbf{\Large Our Approach}

We developed a \textbf{Stochastic Vehicle Routing Problem (SVRP)} framework with reliability constraints, progressing from deterministic baseline (Model I) to chance-constrained optimization (Model II) to two-stage stochastic programming (Model III).

\noindent \textbf{Data-Driven:} Lognormal distributions fitted to 5 years of Somalia road data (147 segments, 1,826 observations per segment).

\noindent \textbf{Algorithm:} Adaptive Large Neighborhood Search (ALNS) with 100 scenarios for optimization, 1,000 for validation.
