\section{Introduction}

Humanitarian organizations face critical challenges delivering life-saving supplies (vaccines, medicines) in regions with unreliable road networks. We address routing optimization for Somalia where travel times are stochastic due to seasonal weather, security incidents, and poor infrastructure.

\subsection{Problem Background}

Traditional vehicle routing assumes deterministic travel times, leading to:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Suboptimal routes:} Shortest paths fail due to road conditions
\item \textbf{Unreliable deliveries:} Communities cannot plan arrivals
\item \textbf{Increased costs:} Emergency re-routing increases expenses 15-30\%
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Problem Statement}

\textbf{Task A (Single Warehouse):} Central warehouse supplies distribution points. Vehicles return to warehouse. Minimize expected time while ensuring reliability $\geq 85\%$.

\textbf{Task B (Multiple Warehouses):} Multiple local warehouses with coordinated strategies, partitioned service areas, and inter-warehouse transfers.

\subsection{Our Approach}

We developed a \textbf{Stochastic Vehicle Routing Problem (SVRP)} framework:

\noindent \textbf{Progressive Models:}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Model I:} Deterministic VRP (expected travel times)
\item \textbf{Model II:} Chance-constrained SVRP (reliability $\alpha = 0.85$)
\item \textbf{Model III:} Two-stage stochastic programming
\end{itemize}

\noindent \textbf{Data-Driven:} Lognormal distributions fitted to 5 years of Somalia road data (147 segments, 1,826 observations).

\noindent \textbf{Algorithm:} Adaptive Large Neighborhood Search (ALNS) with scenario-based sampling (100 optimization, 1,000 validation).
