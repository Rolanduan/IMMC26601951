\subsection{Summary of Key Findings}

This research addresses humanitarian aid delivery route optimization under road network uncertainty in Somalia. Our key contributions:

\textbf{1. Stochastic VRP with Reliability Constraints.} We developed a chance-constrained framework using probability distributions fitted to 5 years of Somalia road data, treating reliability ($\alpha = 0.85$) as a hard constraint rather than cost-focused objective.

\textbf{2. Progressive Modeling Framework.} Model 0 (deterministic) $\to$ Model 1 (chance-constrained) $\to$ Model 2 (two-stage) $\to$ Model 3 (multi-depot) demonstrates systematic understanding and validates components incrementally.

\textbf{3. Effective Algorithm.} ALNS achieves near-optimal solutions (gap $< 4\%$) within 3-4 minutes for realistic instances.

\textbf{4. Multi-Warehouse Coordination.} Clustering-based approach achieves 31\% efficiency improvement vs. centralized distribution.
