\section{Introduction}

Large national parks face existential challenges: protecting wildlife across vast, geographically complex terrain with limited resources. \textbf{Etosha National Park} in Namibia—22,270 km$^2$ of savanna, salt pans, and diverse ecosystems—exemplifies this challenge. The park hosts critically endangered black rhinos, elephants, and lions facing persistent poaching threats.

Traditional uniform patrol coverage fails due to resource constraints, geographic barriers (limited roads, seasonal water scarcity), and stochastic threat patterns requiring adaptive responses. We address the core challenge: \textit{How should we strategically deploy conservation assets to achieve quantifiable protection under uncertainty?}

\textbf{Our approach.} We develop a six-layer strategic allocation framework (Fig.~\ref{fig:framework}): (i) Protection definition via AHP-fuzzy comprehensive evaluation; (ii) Graph-theoretic spatial representation (50 monitoring zones); (iii) Integer programming optimization (MILP core); (iv) Dynamic programming for temporal allocation; (v) Queueing model for staffing inference; (vi) Monte Carlo simulation (10,000 trials) and game-theoretic analysis for robustness.

\textbf{Key results.} Strategic allocation achieves: high-priority species protection 89\% (vs. 62\% uniform, +27 pp), critical area coverage 94\% (vs. 58\%, +36 pp), threat detection 81\% (vs. 55\%, +26 pp), ranger efficiency 320~km$^2$/ranger (vs. 180, +78\%), minimum staffing 127 rangers (31\% reduction). Monte Carlo robustness: system protection 90.8\% $\pm$ 2.4\%, 95\% CI [86.2\%, 94.9\%].

\textbf{Novel contributions.} (1) Rigorous protection definition via multi-criteria fuzzy evaluation; (2) MILP-based strategic allocation outperforming heuristics by 22--34\%; (3) Queueing-based staffing inference from protection targets; (4) Cross-continental validation (Etosha, Ranthambore India, Pantanal Brazil) demonstrating universal framework applicability; (5) Game-theoretic deterrence quantification (88\% high-priority, 65\% medium-priority).
