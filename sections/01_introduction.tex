\section{Introduction}

Large national parks face existential challenges: protecting wildlife across vast, geographically complex terrain with limited resources. \textbf{Etosha National Park} in Namibia—22,935 km$^2$ of savanna, salt pans, and diverse ecosystems—exemplifies this challenge. The park hosts critically endangered black rhinos (∼2,500 in broader ecosystem), elephants, lions, and 114 mammal species facing persistent poaching threats, habitat disruption, and human-wildlife conflict.

\subsection{Problem Restatement: The Core Challenge}

Traditional uniform patrol coverage fails due to resource constraints (295 personnel, 3,551 km roads), geographic barriers (seasonal water scarcity, 4,800 km$^2$ salt pan), and stochastic threat patterns requiring adaptive responses. The International Mission for Monitoring Conservation (IMMC) asks us to address:

\textit{How should we strategically deploy limited conservation assets (rangers, UAVs, camera traps, satellites) across space and time to achieve quantifiable protection under uncertainty?}

The solution must:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Define "protection" in realistic, practical, quantitative terms
    \item Allocate resources strategically (not complete coverage) given constraints
    \item Analyze protection over time and estimate staffing needs
    \item Maintain robustness under resource uncertainty
    \item Adapt to diverse protected areas globally
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Conservation Challenges at Etosha: Prioritized Threats}

Etosha faces multi-dimensional threats requiring differentiated responses:

\textbf{Species-Specific Risks (Priority Ranking):}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textcolor{red}{\textbf{Critical:}} Black Rhinoceros (Endangered) -- Poaching pressure since 2022
    \item \textcolor{orange}{\textbf{High:}} Elephant, Lion (Vulnerable) -- Human-wildlife conflict
    \item \textcolor{yellow}{\textbf{Medium:}} Leopard, Cheetah, Wild Dog -- Habitat fragmentation
    \item \textcolor{green}{\textbf{Low:}} Plains herbivores (zebra, springbok, oryx) -- Natural predation
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Location-Specific Risks:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Waterholes (86 sites):} High aggregation points, predictable poaching targets
    \item \textbf{Salt pan edges (4,800 km$^2$):} Limited accessibility, natural barrier
    \item \textbf{Boundary zones (850 km fence):} Unauthorized entry points
    \item \textbf{Road networks (3,551 km):} Patrol accessibility vs. poacher mobility
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Temporal Risk Patterns:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Diurnal:} Poaching peaks at night (67\% incidents)
    \item \textbf{Seasonal:} Dry season increases wildlife concentration at waterholes
    \item \textbf{Stochastic:** Wildfire outbreaks disrupt large areas unpredictably
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Cross-Continental Validation Preview}

To demonstrate universal applicability (Requirement 6), we validate our framework on reserves across continents:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Africa:} Etosha National Park, Namibia (22,935 km$^2$, rhino/elephant/lion)
    \item \textbf{Asia:} Ranthambore Tiger Reserve, India (1,334 km$^2$, Bengal tiger)
    \item \textbf{South America:} Pantanal Wetlands, Brazil (150,000 km$^2$, jaguar/caiman)
    \item \textbf{North America:} Yellowstone National Park, USA (8,983 km$^2$, wolf/bear)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Key finding:} Identical mathematical structure with location-specific parameters achieves consistent performance: mean protection 89.0\% ($\sigma$ = 3.6\%), mean improvement +28.1 pp.

\subsection{Novel Contributions}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Rigorous protection definition:} Multi-criteria fuzzy evaluation (ecology + threat + operations)
    \item \textbf{MILP-based strategic allocation:} Outperforms heuristics by 22--34\%
    \item \textbf{Queueing-based staffing inference:} Derive minimum personnel from protection targets
    \item \textbf{Cross-continental validation:} Universal framework with 4-continent testing
    \item \textbf{Game-theoretic deterrence:} Quantify adversarial response (88\% high-priority, 65\% medium-priority)
\end{enumerate}
