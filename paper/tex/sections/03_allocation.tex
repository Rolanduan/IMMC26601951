\section{Lexicographic Barrier Design}
\subsection{Decision Roles}
Once residual defense debt \(\Gamma_i\) is known, the managerial question becomes: which assets should physically interrupt or compensate for that debt? We distinguish three roles:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{patrol templates}: repeatable gateway--hinge--return circuits that create physical interception opportunities;
  \item \textbf{sentinel devices}: persistent watch assets placed on high-consequence hinge cells;
  \item \textbf{mobile reserve}: UAV windows held for uncertainty absorption and surge response.
\end{itemize}
The associated decision variables are
\begin{itemize}
  \item \(x_r\in\mathbb{Z}_+\): activations of patrol template \(r\);
  \item \(y_i\in\{0,1,2\}\): number of sentinel devices assigned to cell \(i\);
  \item \(z_j\in\mathbb{Z}_+\): UAV reserve windows assigned to sector \(j\).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Template Library}
Patrol templates are not arbitrary loops. Each template links a station to one or more hinge cells and returns within a feasible shift limit. Let \(a_{ir}=1\) if template \(r\) covers cell \(i\), and \(a_{ir}=0\) otherwise. The duration of template \(r\) is
\begin{equation}
h_r = \sum_{e\in E_R} w_e \xi_e^{(r)} + t_{\mathrm{dwell}} \le h_{\max},
\label{eq:route}
\end{equation}
where \(\xi_e^{(r)}\) indicates whether road edge \(e\) is used and \(t_{\mathrm{dwell}}\) captures on-site watch, reporting, and search behavior. The quantity
\[
F_i=\sum_{r\in\mathcal{R}} a_{ir}x_r
\]
is the patrol-template frequency experienced by cell \(i\).

\subsection{Defense Dose}
The protection dose applied to cell \(i\) is
\begin{equation}
\eta_i=\alpha F_i+\beta y_i+\gamma \sum_j b_{ij}z_j,
\label{eq:dose}
\end{equation}
where \(b_{ij}=1\) if UAV sector \(j\) can reinforce cell \(i\). Instead of maximizing a weighted gain directly, we define the residual debt left after that dose by
\begin{equation}
u_i \ge \Gamma_i-\kappa_i\eta_i,\qquad u_i\ge 0,
\label{eq:residual}
\end{equation}
with \(\kappa_i=\kappa_0+\kappa_1 C_i\) giving additional defensive leverage to actions applied on transport-critical cells.

\subsection{Stage I: Cap the Worst Hinge}
The first optimization stage is a minimax problem:
\begin{equation}
\min \theta
\label{eq:obj_stage1}
\end{equation}
subject to
\begin{equation}
u_i \le \theta,\qquad \forall i\in\mathcal{H},
\label{eq:hingecap}
\end{equation}
where \(\mathcal{H}\) is the hinge-cell set. This stage prevents the controller from producing an attractive average while leaving one decisive cell catastrophically exposed.

\subsection{Stage II: Clean Up Total Residual Debt}
After the optimal hinge ceiling \(\theta^\star\) is found, the second stage minimizes the total residual burden:
\begin{equation}
\min \; Z=\sum_{i=1}^{64}\omega_i u_i + \lambda_h\sum_{r\in\mathcal{R}} h_r x_r + \lambda_u\sum_j z_j,
\label{eq:obj}
\end{equation}
subject to the same feasibility rules and the additional condition \(u_i\le\theta^\star\) for hinge cells. The weights \(\omega_i\) slightly upweight hinge and near-sink cells so that clean-up effort is still pulled toward the most expensive residual pathways.

\subsection{Feasibility Rules}
The design is constrained by manpower, inventory, and station load:
\begin{align}
\sum_{r\in\mathcal{R}} 2h_r x_r &\le H_R, \label{eq:rangerhours}\\
\sum_{i=1}^{64} y_i &\le M, \label{eq:devices}\\
\sum_j z_j &\le U, \label{eq:drones}\\
\sum_{r:s(r)=s} x_r &\le \kappa_s,\quad \forall s, \label{eq:station}\\
u_i &\le \theta^\star,\quad \forall i\in\mathcal{H}.
\label{eq:hotspots}
\end{align}
This is a very different doctrine from maximizing raw weighted coverage. The controller first refuses to leave a hinge disastrously exposed, and only afterward spends any remaining flexibility on average improvement.

\subsection{Operational Interpretation}
The controller therefore produces a three-tier shield:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item protect entry-facing hinges with repeatable patrol templates;
  \item harden biologically expensive connectors with persistent sentinels;
  \item keep part of the UAV capacity mobile so it can relieve whichever sector currently generates the most residual debt.
\end{enumerate}
This design logic is structurally different from corridor saturation. It is closer to barrier placement with a mobile reserve than to broad-area patrol painting.

\subsection{Etosha Barrier Package}
Under baseline assumptions, the resulting design does not spread effort evenly across the eastern--southern corridor. Instead, it allocates most patrol-template activations to the ingress-facing east and the southern relay strip, anchors persistent devices on a smaller hinge subset, and retains aerial flexibility for uncertain sectors that cannot justify permanent assignment.
