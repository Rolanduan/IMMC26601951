\section{Ambiguity-Aware Protection Portfolio}
\subsection{Decision Roles}
Once the liability field \(L_i\) is known, the managerial problem is no longer to block one path at a time. It is to assemble a portfolio that reduces both expected unresolved liability and the risk of a bad operating month. We distinguish three asset roles:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{patrol templates}: repeatable route packages launched from stations;
  \item \textbf{sentinel devices}: persistent monitoring assets pinned to expensive cells;
  \item \textbf{mobile reserve}: UAV windows held for recourse under volatility.
\end{itemize}
The decision variables are
\begin{itemize}
  \item \(x_r\in\mathbb{Z}_+\): activations of patrol template \(r\);
  \item \(y_i\in\{0,1,2\}\): number of sentinel devices assigned to cell \(i\);
  \item \(z_j\in\mathbb{Z}_+\): UAV windows assigned to sector \(j\).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Asset Dictionary}
Patrol templates remain useful, but they are now treated as instruments in a stochastic portfolio. Let \(a_{ir}=1\) if template \(r\) meaningfully services cell \(i\). The route duration is still
\begin{equation}
h_r = \sum_{e\in E_R} w_e \xi_e^{(r)} + t_{\mathrm{dwell}} \le h_{\max},
\label{eq:route}
\end{equation}
but the model no longer optimizes route coverage as an end in itself. Instead, the induced patrol frequency at cell \(i\),
\[
F_i=\sum_{r\in\mathcal{R}} a_{ir}x_r,
\]
enters a probabilistic protection function.

\subsection{Protection Quality and Uncertainty Shrinkage}
Define the effective protection quality on cell \(i\) by
\begin{equation}
p_i(a)
=
1-\exp\!\left(
-\alpha F_i
-\beta y_i
-\gamma\sum_j b_{ij}z_j
\right),
\label{eq:dose}
\end{equation}
where \(b_{ij}=1\) if reserve sector \(j\) can reinforce cell \(i\). Equation~\eqref{eq:dose} is increasing and saturating: additional effort helps, but with diminishing returns.

Assets also reduce ambiguity. We model the remaining information deficit after deployment as
\begin{equation}
s_i(a)
=
U_i\exp\!\left(
-\bar\alpha F_i
-\bar\beta y_i
-\bar\gamma\sum_j b_{ij}z_j
\right).
\label{eq:uncertainty}
\end{equation}
The first exponential reflects deterrence and interception quality; the second reflects information improvement. These two channels are kept separate on purpose.

\subsection{Scenario Loss}
Let \(\Omega\) denote a finite scenario library for dry-season operating conditions, and let \(\xi_{i\omega}\) be the realized liability multiplier for cell \(i\) under scenario \(\omega\). The unresolved ecological cost in scenario \(\omega\) is
\begin{equation}
Y_\omega(a)
=
\sum_{i=1}^{64}\xi_{i\omega}L_i\bigl(1-p_i(a)\bigr)
\;+\;
\psi\sum_{i=1}^{64}s_i(a).
\label{eq:residual}
\end{equation}
The first term is unresolved direct liability; the second is an ambiguity premium. This structure is materially different from the old residual-debt minimax controller.

\subsection{Portfolio Objective}
We optimize a mean--tail objective with an explicit contract penalty:
\begin{align}
\min\;&
\frac{1}{|\Omega|}\sum_{\omega\in\Omega}Y_\omega(a)
\;+\;
\lambda\left(
\eta + \frac{1}{(1-\alpha)|\Omega|}\sum_{\omega\in\Omega}\zeta_\omega
\right)
\;+\;
\phi\sum_{i\in\mathcal{C}}\bigl(\underline p-p_i(a)\bigr)_+
\label{eq:obj}
\\
\text{s.t.}\;&
\zeta_\omega \ge Y_\omega(a)-\eta,\qquad \zeta_\omega\ge 0,\quad \forall \omega\in\Omega.
\label{eq:obj_stage1}
\end{align}
The first term is average unresolved liability; the second is the \(\operatorname{CVaR}_\alpha\) epigraph; the third enforces a minimum protection contract \(\underline p\) on the contract set \(\mathcal{C}\). This is a portfolio problem under ambiguity rather than a single weighted-average deployment rule.

\subsection{Feasibility Rules}
The design is constrained by manpower, inventory, and station departure limits:
\begin{align}
\sum_{r\in\mathcal{R}} 2h_r x_r &\le H_R, \label{eq:rangerhours}\\
\sum_{i=1}^{64} y_i &\le M, \label{eq:devices}\\
\sum_j z_j &\le U, \label{eq:drones}\\
\sum_{r:s(r)=s} x_r &\le \kappa_s,\quad \forall s. \label{eq:station}
\end{align}
This means the optimizer is forced to choose between average performance, tail protection, and contract compliance inside a finite field budget.

\subsection{Operational Interpretation}
The resulting doctrine is different from both route maximization and corridor sealing:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{contract cells} receive guaranteed patrol recurrence or persistent sensors;
  \item \textbf{ridge cells outside the contract set} receive effort according to their contribution to scenario tail risk;
  \item \textbf{mobile reserve} remains uncommitted until volatility at a station or sector justifies intervention.
\end{enumerate}
This is why the paper now reads more like a risk portfolio than like a single controller.

\subsection{Etosha Protection Portfolio}
Under baseline assumptions, the portfolio does not spread effort uniformly over the east--south visual corridor. Instead, it guarantees minimum protection on the liability ridge, uses persistent sensors on the most expensive stable cells, and keeps UAV support flexible so that it can absorb scenario volatility where the marginal reduction in tail risk is largest.
