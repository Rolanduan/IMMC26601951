\section{Trade-Off Frontier and Structural Sensitivity}
The new framework is built around residual loss flow and service reliability, so the natural sensitivity question is no longer ``Which parameter changes the score most?'' but ``Which levers move the system across the stability frontier?'' We define that frontier as
\[
\mathcal{F}=\left\{(n,u,\Delta): \PI \ge 0.70,\ \Gamma_{\max}\ \text{controlled},\ \max_s \Pr(T_s>\tau^\star)\le 0.25 \right\},
\]
where \(n\) is effective team depth, \(u\) is hinge-zone detection uptime, and \(\Delta\) is patrol-template regularity.

This definition creates a different ranking from a raw hardware-count analysis. The dominant levers are:
\[
\text{team depth} > \text{sensor uptime} > \text{template regularity} > \text{device count}.
\]
The distinction between uptime and count is important. Once a hinge zone already has a device, reliability of that device matters more than adding another low-uptime unit somewhere less critical.

\subsection{Team-Depth Shock}
Reducing the field force from 34 to 24 rangers pushes the system across the stability frontier. The main failure is not a graceful drop in average coverage; it is a queueing failure at the busiest station--hinge pairs. Once that happens, the delay exceedance probability rises sharply, which in turn allows residual weighted loss to escape through the same few expensive pathways. In other words, the system fails by congestion before it fails by map area.

\subsection{Sentinel Uptime Loss}
If hinge-zone monitoring uptime deteriorates, the barrier design loses persistence precisely where patrols were meant to be complemented rather than replaced. This explains why uptime behaves as the second-most powerful lever. A park can own enough devices on paper and still be structurally fragile if the biologically decisive hinges are intermittently blind.

\subsection{Template Irregularity}
Patrol-template irregularity matters because the new framework depends on repeatability at gateway and relay cells. When the intended patrol cadence stretches from roughly 1.5 days toward 3 days, residual debt grows even if nominal patrol hours do not collapse. The problem is not only fewer visits; it is longer windows during which a hinge can remain open without a physical barrier or a service backstop.

\subsection{Hardware Inflation Without Structure}
Additional devices remain helpful, but their marginal value falls quickly once the principal hinge set is instrumented. This is why raw device count ranks last among the four levers. Hardware without structural placement or uptime discipline acts like decorative surplus, not like genuine defense depth.

These sensitivity results imply a different policy sequence from a conventional patrol paper. First secure enough teams to keep service reliability inside the frontier. Then protect hinge-zone uptime. Then stabilize patrol regularity. Only after those conditions are met does broader hardware expansion meaningfully improve the system.
