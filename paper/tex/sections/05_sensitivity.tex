\section{CVaR Frontier and Structural Sensitivity}
The new framework is built around unresolved ecological liability, tail risk, and station overload. The correct sensitivity question is therefore not ``Which parameter moves the average score most?'' but ``Which levers move the system across the admissible contract frontier?'' We define that frontier by
\[
\mathfrak{S}
=
\left\{
(n,u,\delta):
\PI\ge 0.72,\;
\operatorname{CVaR}_{0.9}(Y)\le \overline Y,\;
\max_s \Pr(B_s>b^\star)\le 0.20
\right\},
\]
where \(n\) is effective team depth, \(u\) is sentinel uptime on contract cells, and \(\delta\) is the average patrol revisit gap on the liability ridge.

This definition creates a different ranking from a simple hardware-count comparison. The dominant levers are
\[
\text{team depth}
>
\text{sentinel uptime}
>
\text{revisit discipline}
>
\text{raw device count}.
\]
That ordering follows from the new mathematics. Team depth affects overload risk nonlinearly, sentinel uptime changes both protection quality and uncertainty shrinkage on the most expensive cells, and revisit discipline controls whether contract cells repeatedly slip below the minimum standard.

\subsection{Team-Depth Shock}
Reducing the field force from 34 to 24 rangers pushes the system outside \(\mathfrak{S}\). The failure is not a smooth decline in average performance. It is a tail-risk failure. Once station capacity falls below the workload guarantee implied by Equation~\eqref{eq:rangermin}, overload probability rises sharply and the liability premium of bad days expands. This is why team depth remains the first policy priority.

\subsection{Sentinel Uptime Loss}
Contract cells are expensive precisely because late or uncertain decisions matter there. If sentinel uptime on those cells falls materially, the ambiguity premium in Equation~\eqref{eq:residual} rises even if nominal sensor count remains unchanged. This is why uptime outranks count. One reliable device on the correct contract cell is worth more than several unreliable devices placed on lower-liability background cells.

\subsection{Revisit Discipline}
Patrol revisit discipline matters because the portfolio is contract-based. Once the average revisit gap on ridge cells stretches from about 1.5 days toward 3 days, the shortfall term \((\underline p-p_i(a))_+\) is activated for more cells and more scenarios. The result is not merely fewer visits; it is a systematic breach of the intended protection contract.

\subsection{Hardware Inflation Without Structure}
Additional devices still help, but their marginal value diminishes quickly once the contract set \(\mathcal{C}\) is already watched. This is why raw hardware count ranks last among the four major levers. Hardware that is not tied to the contract set or to high-volatility stations behaves like surplus, not like resilience.

The sensitivity lesson is therefore sequential. First preserve enough crews to satisfy the workload guarantee. Then protect uptime on contract cells. Then preserve revisit discipline. Only after those three conditions are secure does broader hardware expansion produce meaningful liability reduction.
