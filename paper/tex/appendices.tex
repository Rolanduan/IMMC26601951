\section{Technical Derivations}
This appendix collects the mathematical pieces behind the re-derived framework so that the manuscript stays readable while the derivations remain explicit.

\subsection{Existence of the Transport Fundamental Matrix}
\begin{proposition}
\label{prop:fundamental}
If the transient transition matrix \(Q\) on the non-sink portion of the transport graph has spectral radius \(\rho(Q)<1\), then the fundamental matrix
\[
N=(I-Q)^{-1}
\]
exists and equals \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}Q^k\).
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
The Neumann series for a square matrix converges when \(\rho(Q)<1\). Therefore
\[
\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}Q^k
\]
converges and satisfies
\[
(I-Q)\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}Q^k = I.
\]
Hence \((I-Q)\) is invertible and its inverse is exactly the convergent series, proving the result.
\end{proof}

Proposition~\ref{prop:fundamental} justifies the transport-criticality score in Equation~\eqref{eq:criticality}. The matrix \(N\) measures expected pre-absorption visits and therefore acts as a principled hinge detector rather than as an ad hoc centrality score.

\subsection{Why the Minimax Stage Controls the Worst Hinge}
\begin{proposition}
\label{prop:minimax}
Let \(\theta^\star\) be the optimum of Stage I under Equation~\eqref{eq:obj_stage1} and constraints \eqref{eq:hingecap}. Then every feasible Stage-II solution that preserves \(u_i\le\theta^\star\) for all \(i\in\mathcal{H}\) weakly controls the worst hinge no worse than the best Stage-I design.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
By construction, Stage I solves
\[
\min \theta \quad \text{s.t.}\quad u_i\le \theta\ \forall i\in\mathcal{H},
\]
over the full feasible region. Therefore no feasible solution can produce a hinge residual bound strictly smaller than \(\theta^\star\). Stage II is solved over the restricted feasible region that additionally enforces \(u_i\le\theta^\star\) for every hinge cell. Every Stage-II solution thus inherits the best achievable upper bound on worst-hinge exposure.
\end{proof}

This proposition is the formal reason the controller differs from a one-stage weighted sum. The model explicitly refuses to buy a better average by sacrificing one decisive hinge.

\subsection{Monotonicity of the Queueing Staffing Rule}
\begin{proposition}
\label{prop:queue}
For fixed arrival rate \(\lambda_s\), service rate \(\mu_s\), and commitment threshold \(\tau^\star\), the delay-exceedance probability in Equation~\eqref{eq:intercept} is nonincreasing in the number of teams \(c_s\) whenever \(\rho_s<1\).
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Increasing \(c_s\) reduces traffic intensity \(\rho_s=\lambda_s/(c_s\mu_s)\), which weakly reduces the Erlang-C waiting term \(P_{\mathrm{wait},s}\). It also weakly increases the decay parameter \(c_s\mu_s-\lambda_s\) in the exponential tail term of Equation~\eqref{eq:intercept}. Both effects move in the same direction, so the resulting delay-exceedance probability cannot increase.
\end{proof}

Proposition~\ref{prop:queue} justifies the staffing floor in Equation~\eqref{eq:rangermin}: the smallest \(c_s\) satisfying the service target is well defined and economically meaningful.

\section*{AI Use Report}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{AI Use Report}
This project uses AI as a drafting and coding copilot, not as an autonomous scientific authority. AI assistance was used to accelerate manuscript structuring, symbolic formatting, figure scripting, and language revision. All equations, assumptions, parameter interpretations, and management conclusions were then reviewed for internal consistency against the intended operating logic of the paper.

The benefit of AI in this workflow is speed across text, code, and figure iteration. The main risk is synthetic coherence without technical closure. For that reason, we used explicit safeguards: consistency checks across model layers, staffing-feasibility checks, equation-to-claim checks, and repeated review of whether the proposed control logic actually matches the park-management interpretation.

Responsibility for the final scientific content remains with the authors.
